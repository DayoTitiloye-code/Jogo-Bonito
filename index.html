Jogo Bonito

"The Beautiful Game"

Jogo bonito is a Portuguese phrase that means the beautiful game and is most often used to describe football.It refers to a creative, skillful, and elegant style of play, strongly associated with Brazil's football culture. The term was popularized worldwide by Pelé and later adopted in marketing and media to celebrate football played with artistry rather than just for results.

Football, also known as soccer, is a team sport played between two teams of eleven players using a spherical ball. The primary objective is to score by getting the ball into the opposing team's net (goal). It is the world's most popular sport, with an estimated 250 million players across over 200 countries

Rules

Each team starts with 11 players. (one goalkeeper and ten outfield players)

A typical game will be 90 minutes long (2, 45 minute halves) excluding extra time.

After the first 45 minutes players will take a 15 minute rest period called half time.

To score the ball must go into your opponent’s goal. The whole ball needs to be over the line for the goal to count.

A goal can be scored with any part of the body apart from the hand or arm up to the shoulder.

Each team can name up to 7 substitute players. Substitutions can be made at any time of the match with each team being able to make a maximum of 3 substitutions per side. (More in some cases)
